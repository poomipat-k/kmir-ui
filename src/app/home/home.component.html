Monitoring Framework for International Relations of Thai Health Promotion Foundation

In assessing the readiness for international relations work, a tailored approach is needed to take into account ThaiHealth's status as an independent state agency, which may impact its decision-making processes, resource allocation, and interactions with external stakeholders. By adapting the assessment methods to capture these unique aspects of ThaiHealth's context, the framework aims to provide a more accurate and relevant assessment of the organisation's readiness for change. As an independent state agency, ThaiHealth has its own unique working style and organisational culture, which must be considered when assessing its readiness for change.



The operationalisation of the framework involves a mixed-methods approach, incorporating both individual and organisational assessments. At the individual level, interviews with academic officers will be conducted to gather insights into their attitudes, beliefs, and capacity. At the organizational level, interviews with heads of sections will be carried out to assess collective commitment and efficacy and gather information on the organization's past and current work. To assess the external environment, a document review will be conducted, which may include an analysis of ThaiHealth's annual reports and relevant WHO documents. Furthermore, the framework acknowledges the importance of ThaiHealth's working style and organizational culture in influencing its readiness for change for international relations works. By conducting interviews with both academic officers and section heads, the framework seeks to understand how these factors may facilitate or hinder the implementation of change initiatives.
